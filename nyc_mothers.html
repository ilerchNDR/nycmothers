The moment to become a mother

In New York City, where you live matters

By Isabel Lerch

Becoming a mother is a milestone in a woman's life. It is an life-changing experience - this is true for every becoming mother. However, when in life this groundbreaking moment happens, differs greatly in a big city such as New York.

While some women are still in high school when giving birth to their first child, others are in their midlife-period having spent already decades in this world. However, most women are in their early thirties when they live the moment to become a mother for the first time.

When looking at data of birth and the maternal age in New York City, two community district stands out: The Upper East Side in Manhatten - a neighborhood which is famous and prominent for its elegance and wealth.

Both districts are home to both ends of the spectrum of maternal age - home to the youngest and the oldest mothers in New York.

The neighborhood is famous and prominent for its elegance and wealth. Depicted in many movies and serie, the Upper East Side is known for its wealthy residents, chic restaurants, and expensive designer shops.

The data shows: Women living on the Upper East Side apparently take their time befire having their first child. Before the age of 30, there are only few women who give birth. And although the majority have their first child at the age 0f 30-34, there is still a significant number of first-time-mothers who are older than 40 years. In fact, the Upper East Side has the highest number of women who are older than 45 years when giving birth for the very first time.

So on the Upper East Side, half of a woman's lifetime happens before a baby is even born. In sharpe contrast, there is another community district in New York City where life seems to happen the other way around: Unionport. Probably a place about which many New Yorkers have been even heard before. Sitting in the south-central section of The Bronx, everyday reality in this neighborhood differs greatly from the life on the Upper East Side.

In Unionport, a neighborhood in the south-central section of The Bronx, women are on average significanlty younger when they give birth to their first child. While the majority of the women who birth here are between 25 and 29 years old, there is a significant number of women who are younger than 20 years old when they give birth for the first time. What stands out significantly is the fact that Unionport has the highest number of first-time-mothers at the age of 15, 16 or 17 years.
